### Section 3: System Board (dark background, white text, 3-column)

**Label:** The coordination layer
**Title:** The architecture between your systems
**Body:** Every industrial operation has three layers. The execution layer: machines, robots, production lines. The data layer: ERP, MES, WMS, the systems that record what happens. And between them, the coordination layer: how signals from the data layer get aggregated into situational awareness, how decisions get routed to the right person with the right context, and how those decisions get pushed back into execution. This layer has been staffed by humans since the first factory. Agent systems are the infrastructure that replaces it.

**Column 1: Signal agents**
- Pull and aggregate data from ERP, MES, WMS, quality systems, and messaging platforms continuously, not on request
- Replace the morning report ritual, the status compilation spreadsheet, the "let me check and get back to you" delay
- Produce a live operational picture that updates itself

**Column 2: Decision agents**
- Pre-assemble the full context package for every decision: financial data, operational impact, historical precedent, risk flags
- Route to the right approver with everything they need to decide in one view
- Surface exceptions and anomalies before they escalate, classified by severity and recommended action

**Column 3: Execution agents**
- Translate approved decisions into downstream actions across systems: trigger purchase orders, update schedules, notify teams, adjust parameters
- Handle the cross-system, cross-team handoffs that currently depend on someone remembering to send an email
- Close the loop between decision and action without human relay

## Academy Page

**Eyebrow:** Academy
**Title:** Building the team that operates alongside agents
**Intro:** Agent systems change what your team does. The reports they used to compile are now automated. The approvals they used to chase are now routed. The data they used to assemble is now aggregated. What remains is the work that actually requires human judgment: supervising agent output, handling exceptions, calibrating trust, extending workflows, and making the decisions that agents surface but cannot make. Academy builds that capability.
**CTA:** Inquire About Training
**Enrollment:** Custom scheduling available for enterprise teams
**Format:** On-site workshop + remote follow-up sessions

### Program 1: Operator Training

**Audience:** Frontline operators and team leads
**Duration:** 2 days on-site + 4 weeks remote support
**Description:** Practical skills for the people who work alongside agent systems on the factory floor and in operations centers every day. How to read agent output, evaluate whether it's correct, recognize when the system has hit the edge of its capability, handle exceptions effectively, and provide the structured feedback that makes the system improve over time.

**Module 1: Agent System Fundamentals (3 hours)**
What agents are, what they're doing behind the scenes, and why they sometimes get it wrong. Mental models for understanding agent behavior without needing to read code. How signals flow from enterprise systems through the agent layer to the outputs you see.

**Module 2: Output Quality Review (3 hours)**
How to evaluate whether an agent's output is correct, complete, and actionable. Calibration exercises using real operational examples from your environment. Building the judgment to trust agent output appropriately, and recognizing the specific patterns that indicate something is wrong.

**Module 3: Exception Handling & Escalation (4 hours)**
Recognizing when an agent has reached the boundary of its capability. The escalation protocols: when to override, when to escalate to a supervisor, when to let the system retry with additional context. Hands-on scenarios built from your actual operational workflows and real edge cases.

**Module 4: Feedback & System Improvement (2 hours)**
How to provide structured feedback that improves agent performance. Logging practices, edge case documentation, and the feedback loop between operators on the floor and the engineering team maintaining the system. Your observations are the training signal that makes the system better.

### Program 2: Management Programs

**Audience:** Operations directors, department heads, and senior managers
**Duration:** 1 day intensive + 6 weeks advisory
**Description:** For the people who set policy, allocate resources, and manage the organizational change that agent systems introduce. Not a technical course. A leadership course on governing autonomous systems in production operations, setting appropriate supervision levels, and managing the transition in team roles and responsibilities.

**Module 1: Agent Systems: Capability and Limits (2 hours)**
What current agent technology can and cannot do in industrial operations. Honest assessment of maturity levels, common failure modes, and the gap between vendor claims and production reality. Where to have confidence and where to maintain skepticism.

**Module 2: Supervision and Governance Design (3 hours)**
Designing supervision policies for agent systems in your operation: autonomy levels by decision type, escalation thresholds, audit requirements, and accountability structures. How to set governance that's rigorous enough to prevent harm and practical enough to capture value.

**Module 3: Deployment Evaluation and Risk (2 hours)**
A framework for evaluating agent deployment proposals from vendors, internal teams, or partners. How to assess technical feasibility, estimate realistic ROI, identify operational risks, and define acceptance criteria that protect your operation and your people.

**Module 4: Organizational Change Management (3 hours)**
Agent systems change job descriptions, team structures, skill requirements, and career paths. How to manage the human dimension: communicating what's changing and why, addressing legitimate concerns, redefining roles to emphasize the judgment work that remains, and building organizational confidence in agent-assisted operations.

### Program 3: Technical Foundations

**Audience:** Software engineers, systems architects, and technical leads
**Duration:** 3 days intensive + 8 weeks project mentorship
**Description:** For engineering teams building or extending agent systems in industrial environments. Covers the full stack from LLM orchestration and prompt engineering through multi-agent coordination architecture to production deployment and operations. Not a lecture series. Participants design, build, and deploy a working agent subsystem during the program using their own operational data.

**Module 1: LLM Orchestration and Prompt Engineering (6 hours)**
Production-grade LLM usage: structured output enforcement, context window management, reliability patterns, retry and fallback strategies, cost optimization. The engineering required to move from "it works in a demo" to "it runs in production at 99%+ accuracy on real operational data."

**Module 2: Tool-Use Protocols and System Integration (6 hours)**
Function Calling, Model Context Protocol (MCP), and custom tool interfaces for enterprise systems. Designing typed tool schemas, handling authentication and authorization flows, building robust integration layers with ERP, MES, WMS, and database systems. Error handling patterns that survive production.

**Module 3: Multi-Agent Coordination Architecture (6 hours)**
The Signal-Decision-Execution coordination pattern. Designing agent topologies that mirror and serve real organizational structures. Inter-agent communication protocols, shared state management, conflict resolution, and comprehensive audit logging for compliance and debugging.

**Module 4: Deployment, Monitoring, and Operations (6 hours)**
Containerized deployment, health monitoring, performance metrics, error tracking, alerting, and graceful degradation. Building agent systems that operations teams can run, debug, and extend without the original developers present. The difference between a demo and a production system.

### Manifesto

**Label:** Why we exist
**Text:** Every era of industrial progress has been driven by the same recognition: this work does not require a human. The power loom. The assembly line. The programmable logic controller. The enterprise resource planning system. Each one automated a layer of industrial operations that had previously required human labor, human attention, or human memory. But one layer was left untouched. The coordination layer, the work of moving information between systems, assembling context for decisions, routing approvals, compiling reports, and relaying execution instructions across teams, has remained manual in even the most automated operations on earth. Not because the technology wasn't ready. Because nobody framed it as an engineering problem. We do. Agent systems are the architectural intervention that brings the coordination layer into the same automated infrastructure as every other layer of modern industrial operations.

### Value Cards

**Card 1: Open by default**
Diagnostic frameworks published openly. Systems built on open architecture. Full source code and operational ownership transferred to the client after every engagement. No vendor lock-in, no proprietary dependencies, no black-box delivery. Our clients run and extend their systems without us. That's the design intent.

**Card 2: Protect human judgment**
Agents handle coordination: data aggregation, context assembly, workflow routing, information relay, and system-to-system handoffs. **Card 3: We build what we recommend**
