# Editorial Columns

懒人原则 Resources is a bilingual publication about AI in industrial operations. Think of it as a TV channel — each column is a "show" with its own angle, audience, and rhythm.

---

## Columns

### 1. `papers` — Paper Trail
**What it is:** Breakdowns of important academic papers and research. Not summaries — analysis. What did the researchers actually find, why does it matter, and what's the gap between the paper and reality.
**Cadence:** 2-3 per month
**Sources:** arXiv, NeurIPS, ICML, ACL, AAAI, journal publications
**Audience:** Technical professionals, researchers, curious generalists

### 2. `tools` — Tool Bench
**What it is:** Honest reviews and deep dives into AI tools, frameworks, platforms, and protocols. What works, what doesn't, what's hype. Hands-on evaluation, not press releases.
**Cadence:** 2-4 per month
**Sources:** Hands-on testing, documentation, GitHub repos, changelogs
**Audience:** Engineers, technical leaders, builders

### 3. `management` — The Org Layer
**What it is:** Management science meets AI. How AI changes decision-making, organizational design, coordination theory, and leadership. Draws from organizational behavior research, operations management, and real deployment experience.
**Cadence:** 2 per month
**Sources:** HBR, management journals, org behavior research, Mintzberg, March, Simon
**Audience:** Managers, directors, executives, MBA-types

### 4. `field` — Field Notes
**What it is:** Dispatches from the real world. What actually happens when you deploy AI in factories, warehouses, and offices. Short, specific, honest. The gap between theory and practice.
**Cadence:** 2-3 per month
**Sources:** Direct observation, practitioner interviews, deployment retrospectives
**Audience:** Operations people, practitioners, anyone curious about ground truth

### 5. `fundamentals` — From Scratch
**What it is:** Explainers for people who want to understand AI from first principles. No jargon gatekeeping. What are agents? How do LLMs actually work? What's the difference between automation and intelligence? Written for smart people who aren't AI engineers.
**Cadence:** 1-2 per month
**Sources:** Textbooks, foundational papers, clear thinking
**Audience:** General professionals, public, anyone entering the field

### 6. `china` — China Desk
**What it is:** AI developments, policy, industry moves, and cultural context specific to China. Written from inside the ecosystem, not as an outside observer.
**Cadence:** 2 per month
**Sources:** 36Kr, InfoQ China, government policy docs, industry reports, Zhihu discussions
**Audience:** Chinese professionals, international observers of Chinese tech

### 8. `us` — US Desk
**What it is:** Silicon Valley, Washington DC, and the American factory floor. AI industry dynamics, federal and state regulation, enterprise adoption patterns, and the cultural context of AI deployment in the US. The counterpart to China Desk — written with insider knowledge, not outsider curiosity.
**Cadence:** 2 per month
**Sources:** TechCrunch, The Information, NIST AI frameworks, White House executive orders, industry analyst reports, practitioner communities
**Audience:** American professionals, Chinese companies expanding to the US, anyone watching the US AI landscape

### 9. `policy` — Signal & Noise
**What it is:** AI policy, regulation, industry trends, and macro developments. Separating signal from noise in a world drowning in AI takes. Short, opinionated, well-sourced.
**Cadence:** 2-4 per month
**Sources:** Government announcements, industry reports, regulatory filings, Gartner/McKinsey/Forrester
**Audience:** Decision-makers, policy watchers, strategists
